# Fume Extractors

Soldering heats flux and creates a plume that should be captured before it reaches the
breathing zone. Rosin-based solder flux fume is a recognized cause of occupational
asthma; a desk fan that merely blows the plume around the room is not extraction.

airflow, replacement filters, and the distance at which capture is effective.

### FA-400 and factory/warehouse listings

FA-400-format desktop absorbers are commonly offered by factory or warehouse sellers
at lower prices. Distinguish three cases:

1. Genuine HAKKO FA-400 from an identifiable HAKKO seller or distributor.
2. A clearly labelled FA-400-compatible or FA-400-style unit with its own manufacturer.
3. An unbranded look-alike using copied HAKKO photographs or model claims.

The genuine HAKKO FA-400 can operate horizontally or vertically, is marked ESD-safe
by HAKKO, and uses a replaceable filter. Verify against the
[official FA-400 product page](https://www.hakko.com/english/products/detail.php?s_url=hakko_fa400_set).
A Malaysian Shopee listing was also indexed for
[Hakko Products Sdn Bhd's FA-400](https://shopee.com.my/HAKKO-FA-400-Desktop-Solder-Smoke-Absorber-Fume-Extractor-220-240V-Soldering-Accessories-Tools-DIY-i.780293996.19730232471).

Factory/warehouse alternatives can be reasonable budget choices, but call them
FA-400-style unless manufacturer authenticity is verified. Confirm 220-240 V input,
filter dimensions, replacement-filter supply, airflow, motor duty, warranty, and ESD
claims. Do not pay a brand premium for a copied label.

### Replacement filters

HAKKO lists `A1001` as the genuine FA-400 replacement filter set, supplied as five
filters. Keep at least one unopened set before the installed filter becomes loaded:

- [Official HAKKO FA-400 replacement parts](https://www.hakko.com/english/products/hakko_fa400_parts.html)
- [Search Shopee Malaysia for HAKKO A1001 filters](https://shopee.com.my/search?keyword=hakko%20a1001%20filter)
- [Search Shopee Malaysia for FA-400 replacement filters](https://shopee.com.my/search?keyword=fa-400%20replacement%20filter)

For an FA-400-style unit, measure the filter and record the unit manufacturer before
ordering. Similar-looking pads can differ in thickness, density, airflow resistance,
and sorbent content. A generic pad is not a genuine `A1001` filter unless provenance
and packaging support that claim.

Replace a filter according to its manual and sooner when it is physically loaded,
damaged, or no longer controls the plume/odour as expected. The FA-400 filter is not
a reason to describe the desktop unit as HEPA filtration. For higher-exposure or
recirculating work, select extraction based on documented capture and filtration.

## Preferred control order

1. Capture fumes close to the solder joint with local exhaust ventilation.
2. Discharge contaminated air safely outdoors where practical.
3. If air is recirculated, use a unit designed for solder fume with appropriate
   particle and gas/odour filtration and available replacement filters.
4. Maintain good room ventilation and keep the operator's face out of the plume.
5. Reduce overheating and use the lowest effective iron temperature.

The UK Health and Safety Executive states that a soldering booth is best and that
bench boxes are of limited use unless fitted with a HEPA filter. It also recommends
keeping the head out of the plume and maintaining extraction. See
[HSE solderer guidance](https://www.hse.gov.uk/asthma/solderers.htm) and
[Solder fume and you](https://www.hse.gov.uk/pubns/indg248.htm).

## Buying checklist

- Flexible arm or hood that can sit close to the joint
- Airflow stated at the working distance, not only at the bare fan
- Replaceable prefilter and main particle filter
- Activated-carbon or other specified gas-phase filter where air recirculates
- Replacement filters sold separately and affordable locally
- Filter-change indicator or a written maintenance interval
- Stable base, acceptable noise, and correct 230 V Malaysian power input
- ESD-safe construction when used directly over sensitive assemblies

HEPA captures particles; a thin carbon sheet has limited capacity for vapours. A
listing that says only "99.9% purification" without a test method, filter grade, or
replacement part number is not enough evidence.

## Placement test

Place the inlet close enough that the visible plume moves into the hood immediately
without passing the operator's face. Cross-drafts can defeat capture. Reposition the
hood whenever the work location moves.

Do not use tissue smoke, solvents, or deliberate burning as an indoor airflow test.
Observe normal solder plume briefly from outside the breathing path, or use a safe
airflow indicator intended for ventilation checks.

## Maintenance

- Inspect the inlet and prefilter frequently.
- Replace filters according to loading and manufacturer guidance.
- Do not wash disposable HEPA or carbon filters unless explicitly permitted.
- Clean flux deposits from arms and housings with the unit unplugged.
- Record filter changes and keep replacements in a sealed, dry bag.
- Stop and reassess if soldering causes coughing, wheezing, chest tightness, or irritation.
