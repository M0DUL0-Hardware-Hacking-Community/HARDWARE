# Spudger Sets

Spudgers and opening tools disconnect small connectors, route wires, release clips,
and separate housings without using a conductive screwdriver near electronics.

Catalog guidance reviewed: **12 July 2026**.

Spudger and opening-pick sets are commonly cheapest from tool factories or warehouse
sellers. Prefer listings with close photographs of moulding quality, material and
thickness, exact piece count, and customer images after use. Buy spare plastic picks;
they are consumables and should be replaced when edges become sharp or cracked.

## Useful starter set

- Glass-filled or fibre-reinforced nylon pointed/flat spudger
- Thin plastic opening picks with depth marks
- Wider plastic opening tool for clips
- Suction cup for screens and smooth panels
- ESD-safe blunt and fine tweezers
- Plastic cards for broad adhesive separation
- Small organiser so worn tools are not mixed with good ones

iFixit describes its glass-filled nylon spudger as antistatic and suitable around
sensitive components, with flat, pointed, and wire-hook functions. Its repair guidance
recommends ESD-safe spudgers around connectors and boards instead of flat screwdrivers.
See [iFixit spudger](https://www.ifixit.com/products/spudger) and
[tool guidance](https://edu.ifixit.com/tools-and-materials).

## Listing checks

- Material identified, not just "plastic"
- Smooth moulding without sharp flash or brittle translucent edges
- Thin tools include depth markings to reduce battery/cable puncture risk
- Replaceable picks rather than one thick universal pry bar
- Tweezers state material and ESD treatment
- Recent reviews show the exact selected kit, not a different variation

Marketplace claims of "ESD safe" are difficult to verify. Use reputable tools for
valuable exposed electronics and reserve unknown plastics for powered-off mechanical
opening work.

## Safe use

1. Power down, disconnect chargers, and remove batteries when possible.
2. Check a repair guide for hidden screws, cables, clips, and adhesive.
3. Limit insertion depth around lithium pouch batteries and display cables.
4. Pry gradually at several clips instead of twisting one point aggressively.
5. Use plastic around live connectors; reserve metal tools for controlled tasks.
6. Never lever directly against small components or unsupported PCB edges.
7. Replace cracked, burred, melted, or heavily sharpened plastic tools.

Metal spudgers are conductive, can scratch traces, and can puncture batteries. They
are not substitutes for ESD-safe nylon tools and should not be used blindly inside a
device.
