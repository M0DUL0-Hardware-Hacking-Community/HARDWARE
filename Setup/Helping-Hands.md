# Helping Hands and PCB Holders

A holder keeps the board stable, reduces strain on pads and wires, and keeps hands
away from a hot iron. The correct fixture depends on board size, connector access,
and whether the work is soldering, probing, inspection, or photography.

## Recommended types

| Type | Best use | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Spring-rail PCB holder | Flat boards and through-hole assembly | Edge clamps can block connectors |
| Weighted PCB vise | Small boards and repeated rework | Limited board width |
| Magnetic flexible arms | Wires, connectors, odd shapes, camera work | Cheap arms can drift under load |
| Articulated alligator clips | Very low-cost wire holding | Bare teeth damage wires and boards |
| Inspection stand with magnifier | Visual inspection and coarse work | Poor optics or ring lights can obstruct tools |

## Buying checklist

- Heavy, non-slip base that does not tip with every arm extended
- Heat-resistant arms and joints that hold position
- Replaceable silicone clip covers or smooth jaws
- Enough clearance to solder both sides without unclamping live wires
- Board clamps that avoid components and do not flex the PCB
- ESD-conscious materials or a verified bonding arrangement at an ESD workstation
- Magnifier with usable working distance, not only high advertised magnification
- Standard screws/threads so arms and clips can be replaced

Avoid uninsulated steel alligator teeth directly on PCB traces, battery pouches,
coaxial cables, or fine insulation. Silicone covers reduce scratching but are not proof
of ESD safety or high-temperature suitability.

## Safe use

1. De-energize and discharge the board before clamping it.
2. Clamp on mechanically supported board edges, not components or flex cables.
3. Route cables so a snag cannot pull the hot iron or board off the bench.
4. Keep arms clear of the iron stand and fume-extractor inlet.
5. Support heavy connectors separately instead of loading solder pads.
6. Check that metal fixtures cannot short battery terminals or powered test points.
