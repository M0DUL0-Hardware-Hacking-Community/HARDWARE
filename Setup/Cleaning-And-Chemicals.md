# Cleaning and Chemicals

Electronics cleaning removes flux, grease, dust, corrosion residue, and thermal
compound without damaging plastics, coatings, displays, labels, adhesives, or the
operator. No solvent is universally safe for every assembly.

## IPA selection

Use IPA with a known concentration and supplier documentation. High-purity IPA is
commonly preferred for flux and grease because it introduces less water and generally
dries faster than 70-75% disinfectant formulations. The right concentration still
depends on the contaminant, component, coating, and cleaning process.

## Bottle and dispenser rules

- Keep bulk IPA in its original labelled container with the supplier SDS available.
- Decant only a small working quantity.
- Label the working bottle with chemical, concentration, hazards, and fill date.
- Verify the bottle, seals, tube, and pump are compatible with IPA.
- Use an ESD-safe dispenser where charge control is required and the claim is verified.
- Never use food/drink bottles or a container that could be mistaken for water.
- Replace bottles that leak, craze, swell, self-pump, lose labels, or collect contamination.

## Cleaning procedure

1. Disconnect every power source and remove batteries when practical.
2. Identify the contaminant and check component/material compatibility.
3. Work with extraction or ventilation and remove ignition sources.
4. Test an inconspicuous area before cleaning broadly.
5. Apply the minimum liquid needed; do not flood switches, microphones, displays, or
   unsealed electromechanical parts.
6. Use clean brushes/swabs so residue is removed rather than redistributed.
7. Allow the assembly and trapped spaces to dry completely before applying power.
8. Inspect under magnification for residue, fibres, corrosion, and displaced parts.

IPA is flammable. Keep it away from a powered soldering iron, hot-air station, sparks,
flames, and hot surfaces. Do not spray solvent into energized equipment. Follow the
supplier SDS for ventilation, PPE, storage, spill response, and disposal.

## Contact cleaner and other solvents

"Contact cleaner" is not one universal chemistry. Confirm whether a product is
plastic-safe, residue-free, lubricating, flammable, and intended for energized use.
Do not substitute acetone, paint thinner, petrol, or unknown degreaser on electronics.

Specialised conformal-coating removers, adhesive removers, and thermal-paste cleaners
should remain in their original containers and be used only with their SDS and a
material-compatibility plan.

## Maintenance and storage

- Keep containers tightly closed in a cool, dry, ventilated location away from ignition.
- Maintain only the quantity needed for normal work.
- Inspect bottles, labels, pumps, and caps monthly.
- Separate clean swabs/brushes from contaminated tools.
- Replace brushes that shed, deform, or retain conductive debris.
- Dispose of chemicals and contaminated materials through appropriate local routes.
